## Design Context

### Users
**Primary:** Electrical/electronic manufacturers evaluating a rep agency to represent their product line. They are VP Sales, National Sales Managers, or Regional Directors. They browse this site from a laptop in an office, comparing 3–5 rep agencies. They want to know: can these people actually sell my product in the Upper Midwest?

**Secondary:** Electrical distributors, contractors, and specifying engineers looking for product support. They need a phone number, a contact name, and proof that ECS knows their market.

**Tertiary:** End users with a specific application question who were referred by a distributor.

### Brand Personality
**Three words:** Weathered · Precise · Weighted

ECS has been doing this since 1967 — before most of their competitors existed. They're not flashy. They're not trying to impress with design trends. They want to communicate: we have the relationships, we know the market, we've been here, we'll still be here. Think a precision instrument that has been well cared-for. A spec sheet from a premium manufacturer. A firm handshake from someone who's done this for decades.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme:** Primarily light (B2B audience, office context, daytime browsing) with dark hero and dark accent sections
- **Typography:** Big Shoulders Display (headings) + Chivo (body) — industrial authority + structured precision
- **Color:** Forest green palette derived from client logo. OKLCH hue 140°. No secondary accent colors — the green IS the brand. Use shade variation for hierarchy, not additional hues.
- **Layout:** Editorial. Left-aligned. Asymmetric. Not centered. Not cards. Think industrial product catalog meets premium trade publication.
- **Imagery:** Professional/industrial. Real situations. Not abstract.
- **Motion:** Restrained. Scroll-triggered reveals only. Nothing that bounces or spins.

### Anti-references
- NOT: Any of the competitor sites (Rouzer, MRL, GJ Sales, Core4, EMS Partners) — all outdated, cluttered, or generic
- NOT: Startup/tech company aesthetics (no gradients, no glassmorphism, no neon)
- NOT: Card grids (every AI site uses them)

### Design Principles
1. **Trust through age** — 1967 should be visible. It's not a footnote. It's the single strongest trust signal.
2. **Editorial not transactional** — layouts should feel like a premium trade magazine, not an e-commerce site
3. **Specificity earns credibility** — name the markets (Medical, Military, Industrial). Name the manufacturers. Name the person to call. Vagueness reads as weakness.
4. **Dark green is authority** — use it for moments that matter: hero, key CTAs, section headers. Don't dilute it.
5. **Whitespace is confidence** — a company that's been around 57 years doesn't need to cram everything above the fold
